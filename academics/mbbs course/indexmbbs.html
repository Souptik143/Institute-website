Cardiology



Aim is to ensuring the provision of evidence-based comprehensive cardiac care.

Improving the quality of life through holistic cardiac health care practices with dedicated approach to new learnings and inculcating evidence based competencies among health care professionals.

Objectives:
• To provide research oriented globally standardised cardiac care.

• To train the qualified health care professionals for delivering superlative care and making precautionary awareness related to heart diseases and treatment.

Anaesthesiology



The anaesthesiologists are not only concerned with the administration of anaesthesia during surgery but are also involved in assessing the underlying comorbidities of the patients and optimizing them before surgery, managing postoperative pain through various modalities, providing intensive care to the critical patients, providing life-saving resuscitation if needed and managing chronic pain as well as palliation in end-stage disorders.

Anaesthesiologists today are trained to cater to the unique needs and manage the consequent risks of a range of surgical and medical specialties, from high-risk surgeries to daycare procedures. Changing patient populations, expanding locations of care, multiple comorbidities, extremes of age, non-communicable epidemics like obesity and addiction as well as pandemics like COVID-19 pose ever-increasing challenges to this specialty.
